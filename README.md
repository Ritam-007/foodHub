## Overview
FoodHub is a mobile food ordering application designed to provide users with a smooth, intuitive, and visually engaging experience while browsing restaurants and ordering meals online.

This project focuses on solving usability and navigation problems commonly found in food delivery apps, especially for first-time users.

---

## Problem Statement
Many food ordering applications overwhelm users with cluttered interfaces, complex navigation, and unclear checkout flows.  
Users often face:

- Difficulty finding restaurants quickly
- Confusing cart & checkout process
- Too many steps to place an order
- Poor mobile usability

The goal was to design a **simple, clean, and fast ordering experience**.

---

## Target Users
- College students
- Working professionals
- First-time online food ordering users
- Users ordering from mobile devices

---

## Design Goals
- Reduce ordering steps
- Improve readability
- Create a friendly interface
- Fast checkout process
- Mobile-first design

---

## My Design Process

### 1. Research
I studied popular food apps like:
- Swiggy
- Zomato
- Uber Eats

I analyzed:
- navigation patterns
- cart flow
- payment process
- onboarding experience

---

### 2. Wireframing
Low-fidelity wireframes were created to focus on structure instead of visuals.

Key focus:
- Simple home page
- Quick restaurant access
- Easy cart visibility
- Minimal checkout steps

---

### 3. UI Design
I designed a clean and modern interface using:

- clear typography
- consistent spacing
- primary accent color
- high contrast buttons

I followed **mobile usability principles** and thumb-friendly interactions.

---
